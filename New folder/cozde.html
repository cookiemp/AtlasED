Understanding Vectors & Scalars

In physics, quantities are classified into two main categories: vectors and scalars. Understanding the distinction between these two is fundamental to describing motion and forces accurately.

Scalar Quantities

A scalar quantity is defined solely by its magnitude. It does not have a direction associated with it. Common examples include mass, temperature, time, and speed.

lightbulb

Key Insight

Speed is scalar (50 mph), while Velocity is a vector (50 mph North).

Vector Quantities

Unlike scalars, a vector quantity possesses both magnitude and direction. To fully describe a vector, you must specify which way it is pointing. Examples include displacement, velocity, acceleration, and force.

Formula Vector Addition

R = A + B

Where R is the resultant vector.

Vectors are typically represented graphically by arrows. The length of the arrow corresponds to the magnitude of the vector, and the arrowhead points in the direction of the vector.
